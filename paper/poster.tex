%% ── INTRODUCTION & MOTIVATION ─────────────────────────────
\begin{tcolorbox}[sectiontitle]
  \vphantom{Ag} Introduction \& Motivation
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{4pt}
\begin{tcolorbox}[posterbox]
Between 2000 and 2019, floods affected \textbf{1.65 billion people} and caused
over US\$650 billion in economic damage (UNDRR, 2020). Near-real-time flood extent
maps are essential for emergency logistics, yet optical satellite imagery is routinely
blocked by cloud cover during active flood events. Sentinel-1 C-band SAR penetrates
clouds and operates day/night, but deep learning models trained on one region
\textbf{generalise poorly to unseen regions}—the \textit{out-of-distribution (OOD)}
generalisation problem.

\vspace{6pt}
The Height Above Nearest Drainage (HAND) index encodes a domain-constant physical
law: water cannot accumulate at elevations far above the nearest drainage channel.
Encoding this constraint as a \textbf{hard architectural prior} rather than a learned
feature should improve OOD-stable inductive bias.

\vspace{8pt}
\textbf{Research Questions}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt,topsep=2pt]
  \item[\color{dkuTeal}\textbf{RQ1.}]
    Does a multiplicative physics gate outperform HAND concatenation for
    OOD SAR flood segmentation?
  \item[\color{dkuTeal}\textbf{RQ2.}]
    Do MC Dropout and test-time augmentation provide actionable, well-calibrated
    flood uncertainty?
  \item[\color{dkuTeal}\textbf{RQ3.}]
    Can uncertainty-stratified flood predictions support population-exposure
    triage for humanitarian response?
\end{itemize}

%% ── MATERIALS AND METHODS ─────────────────────────────────
\begin{tcolorbox}[sectiontitle]
  \vphantom{Ag} Materials and Methods
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{4pt}
\begin{tcolorbox}[posterbox]
\textbf{SAR imagery.} Sen1Floods11 (Bonafilia et al. 2020): 446 co-registered
Sentinel-1 VV/VH chips, $512\!\times\!512\,\text{px}$ at 10\,m across 14 flood
events covering 11 ecoregions. Bolivia (15 chips, 2,867,815 pixels) withheld
as geographically disjoint test set.

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{Terrain.} HAND computed from MERIT Hydro DEM (Yamazaki et al. 2019);
90\,m, resampled to 10\,m. Normalised per-chip; $\mu{=}9.35\,\text{m}$,
$\sigma{=}28.33\,\text{m}$. Exported via Google Earth Engine JavaScript API.

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{Population.} WorldPop 2020 unconstrained estimates at 1\,arc-sec
(Stevens et al. 2015), reprojected to match SAR chip CRS (WGS84 / EPSG:32720).

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{Class imbalance.} Train ${\sim}4\%$ flood; Bolivia test ${\sim}12\%$
reflecting real-world rarity of open-water pixels.

\vspace{8pt}
\begin{center}
  \includegraphics[width=0.97\linewidth]{dataset.jpeg}
  \captionof{figure}{\textbf{Fig. 2} TerrainFlood-UQ dataset: SAR VV diversity
  across 9 of 11 global ecoregions used in training.}
\end{center}

\vspace{6pt}
\textbf{Encoder.} Weight-shared Siamese ResNet-34 processes pre-event and
post-event SAR branches to produce aligned change-sensitive feature maps
$F_\text{pre}$ and $F_\text{post}$.

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{HAND gate.} $\alpha(h) = \exp(-h/50)$. Differentiable; zero learnable
parameters; globally applicable via MERIT Hydro. Suppresses flood logits wherever
terrain physics preclude inundation ($\alpha \!\approx\! 0$ at $h > 150\,\text{m}$).

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{Uncertainty.} MC Dropout ($p{=}0.2$, $T{=}20$ passes) and TTA over D$_4$
symmetry group (8 augmentations). Post-hoc temperature scaling ($T{=}0.10$).

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{Training.} BCE ($\omega{=}10$) + Dice loss; Adam ($\text{lr}{=}1\!\times\!
10^{-4}$); batch=8; patience=25; max 120 epochs. NVIDIA L20 48\,GB GPU (DKUCC cluster).

\vspace{5pt}
\textbf{Ablation study.} Four designs (A–D) test each design choice:
baseline, HAND concat, physics gate, gate+MC Dropout. Full retraining
(patience=25) distinguishes training-time convergence from architectural effects.

  ResNet-34 encoder; HAND gate logit modulation; U-Net decoder; MC Dropout
  + TTA uncertainty heads.}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

%% ── DISCUSSION ────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{tcolorbox}[sectiontitle]
  \vphantom{Ag} Discussion
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{4pt}
\begin{tcolorbox}[posterbox]

\textbf{I. Why the gate dominates concatenation (+22.1 pp).} With only 431
training chips, gradient-based learning cannot reliably discover the HAND–flood
anti-correlation from raw input channels (Variant B). The gate encodes this
constraint unconditionally as $\alpha(h){\in}(0,1]$, suppressing logits wherever
terrain physics preclude inundation regardless of SAR signal, and thereby providing
an OOD-stable inductive bias.

\vspace{7pt}
\textbf{II. MC Dropout failure mode (novel finding).} MC Dropout variance
($\bar{\sigma}^2{=}4{\times}10^{-4}$) is three orders of magnitude below
actionable thresholds, and its uncertainty–error correlation is inverted
($r{=}{-}0.815$, AURC$=0.517{\approx}$random). The gate collapses all logit
magnitudes into $[0,\alpha\cdot L]$, eliminating stochastic mask diversity
across $T{=}20$ passes. TTA, which perturbs input geometry rather than model
weights, is immune to this mechanism ($\bar{\sigma}^2{=}8.3{\times}10^{-3}$,
$r{=}+0.614$, AURC$=0.421$). This generalises to \textit{any} ML system with
output-space physics constraints.

\vspace{7pt}
\textbf{III. Calibration and deployment.} The gate compresses logits, producing
systematic under-confidence (pre-calibration ECE$=0.363$, Brier$=0.195$).
Temperature scaling ($T{=}0.10$) restores ECE$=0.063$ and Brier$=0.053$
($-78.6\%$ and $-72.8\%$ respectively). Physics-gated models \textit{must not}
be deployed without post-hoc calibration. Optimal decision threshold $\tau^*{=}0.5$
confirms the calibrated probabilities recover a proper Bayesian interpretation.

\vspace{7pt}
\textbf{IV. Population exposure triage.} Of the 7.84 M people within the
D\textsubscript{full} flood extent (Bolivia test set, WorldPop 2020), TTA
uncertainty flags \textbf{1.21 M people (15.4\%)} in ambiguous boundary zones
where $\sigma^2{>}0.01$. These zones should be prioritised for field verification
as they represent the highest marginal benefit of ground-truth acquisition relative
to model cost.

\vspace{7pt}
\textbf{V. Limitations.} Evaluation limited to one OOD event (Bolivia 2018
flooding). HAND at 90\,m resampled to 10\,m introduces sub-pixel terrain artefacts.
Wet-snow and urban targets may still confuse the SAR signal even after gating.
Bootstrap CIs (${\sim}30$\,pp wide) reflect the small 15-chip test set.

%% ── CONCLUSIONS ───────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{tcolorbox}[sectiontitle]
  \vphantom{Ag} Conclusions
\end{tcolorbox}
\vspace{4pt}
\begin{tcolorbox}[findingbox]
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=4pt,topsep=2pt]
  \item[\color{successGreen}\large\textbf{1.}]
    \textbf{Encoding terrain physics as a hard architectural gate}—not as a
    learned input—yields a +22.1 pp OOD IoU gain (0.408 → 0.724). The gate
    is globally valid, requiring no per-region retraining.

  \item[\color{successGreen}\large\textbf{2.}]
    \textbf{Temperature scaling is non-optional} for physics-gated flood models:
    the gate compresses logit range, causing severe under-confidence (ECE$=0.363$)
    that is fully corrected post-hoc (ECE$=0.063$, $-78.6\%$).

  \item[\color{successGreen}\large\textbf{3.}]
    \textbf{TTA over D$_4$ is the correct uncertainty estimator} for gated
    architectures: $r{=}+0.614$, variance $20{\text{–}}50\times$ MC Dropout.
    MC Dropout's inverted signal ($r{=}{-}0.815$) is a structural artifact of
    gate-induced logit suppression—\textit{a novel failure mode requiring
    disclosure before operational deployment}.

  \item[\color{dkuGold}\large\textbf{4.}]
    \textbf{Uncertainty-aware exposure triage} is operationally feasible:
    1.21 M of 7.84 M at-risk people (15.4\%) can be flagged for field
    verification using TTA $\sigma^2 > 0.01$, reducing verification cost by
    focusing limited resources on uncertain flood boundaries.

  \item[\color{highlightBlue}\large\textbf{5.}]
    \textbf{Next steps:} Multi-event OOD evaluation (monsoon, snowmelt, storm
    surge); ensemble uncertainty replacing MC Dropout; automated HAND pipeline
    for near-real-time deployment; uncertainty-aware evacuation routing dashboard.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}
